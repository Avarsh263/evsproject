Causes of Soil Pollution

Understanding the root causes of soil pollution is crucial for effective mitigation strategies.

Industrial Waste

The disposal of industrial waste, including heavy metals and other toxic substances, significantly contaminates the soil.

Learn More

Agricultural Chemicals

The use of pesticides, fertilizers, and other agricultural chemicals can lead to soil pollution, reducing soil biodiversity and fertility.

Improper Waste Disposal

The improper disposal of both hazardous and non-hazardous waste contributes significantly to soil contamination.

Mining Activities

Mining processes, such as acid mine drainage, can release toxic substances into the soil and water.
